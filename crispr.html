What is CRISPR-Cas9?

CRISPR-Cas9 is a molecular "scissors" system that can precisely cut DNA at targeted locations. A guide RNA (gRNA) directs the Cas9 protein to a specific sequence in the genome, where it makes a double-strand break.

The Cas9 protein requires a short motif next to the target site called the PAM sequence (NGG) — without it, Cas9 cannot bind or cut.

Algorithm Used

  1. 1. Clean input — uppercase, remove non-ATGC characters
  2. 2. Scan left-to-right: find positions where [N]GG appears at position i+20, i+21
  3. 3. Extract 20nt immediately before the PAM as the guide RNA
  4. 4. Cut site = between positions 17 and 18 of the guide RNA
  5. 5. Generate reverse complement: A↔T, G↔C, then reverse the string
  6. 6. Repeat scan on reverse complement strand
